# Microsoft Corporation - Form 10-K - Fiscal Year Ended June 30, 2023

## Item 1. Business

Microsoft Corporation develops, licenses, and supports a wide range of software products, services, and devices. The Company's segments include Productivity and Business Processes, Intelligent Cloud, and More Personal Computing.

## Item 1A. Risk Factors

Key risks include, but are not limited to:
- Intense competition in all of Microsoft's markets.
- Cybersecurity threats and data privacy concerns.
- Reliance on innovation and ability to respond to technological changes.
- Risks associated with acquisitions and strategic investments.

## Item 7. Management's Discussion and Analysis of Financial Condition and Results of Operations

**Overview:**
For the fiscal year ended June 30, 2023, Microsoft reported revenue of $211.9 billion, an increase of 7% (11% in constant currency) compared to $198.3 billion in the prior year. This growth was driven by strong performance in the Intelligent Cloud segment, particularly Azure and other cloud services. Operating income was $88.5 billion, and net income was $72.4 billion. Diluted earnings per share were $9.68.

**Segment Performance:**
- **Productivity and Business Processes:** Revenue of $69.3 billion, up 10% (14% in constant currency). Growth driven by Office 365 Commercial and Dynamics 365.
- **Intelligent Cloud:** Revenue of $91.9 billion, up 19% (23% in constant currency). Azure and other cloud services revenue grew 27% (31% in constant currency).
- **More Personal Computing:** Revenue of $50.7 billion, down 13% (10% in constant currency). Windows OEM revenue decreased, and Devices revenue also declined. Xbox content and services revenue grew.
